Making the Correct Decision

Detailed analysis and careful consideration is key in decision making.
And decisions are the key to action.

Improved efficiency is going to be paramount in keeping costs manageable as the service area and customer base continues to grow. Historically there has been a negative impact on customer satisfaction as new regions are opened for customer service and the satisfaction dose not always recover to its previous values over time. If this trend were to continue then the cost of power that comes from consumer conservation would eventually reach prices similar to that of other power sources because it would require more spending to convince consumers to participate in conservation programs. By centralizing and improving the process the trend can be revised and the customer satisfaction ratings can begin to increase, ultimately further reducing the spending required to convince consumers to participate in conservation programs.

Evaluation of the Options

The proposed changes to the business process is not the only option for improvement. The scheduling of service request could be subcontracted out and therefore variableizing the cost. Costs associated with the turn over to a subcontractor are less than the upfront costs of changing the process within The Company, but the subcontractor process does not have as high an impact on customer satisfaction. Evaluation of the different options and the probabilities of impact on customer satisfaction is used to evaluate the possible outcomes and value of each option.

Decision Determination

Based upon the analysis performed on the options available the proposed business model was recommended. Dynamic simulation showed that there is an approachable impact on revenue from the customer satisfaction rating, indicating that efforts to improve that rating will improve revenue for The Company. Event simulation was used to identify how best to maximize the customer satisfaction showing that decreasing time on calls has the most direct impact on the rating. Analysis of the operational process identified what the operation needed to be focused on and where shortcomings in the existing implementation existed. The costs were determined and a plan for implementation was established that showed the costs over time and when benefit would be attained from the proposed change. All of this information, along with probabilities of success, was compared against other alternatives in order to determine if it was the best path for improving the customer satisfaction and revenue. This decision analysis identified that the proposed business model had the highest expected value and was selected by this project to be proposed and the choice that The Company should make.
