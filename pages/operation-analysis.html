The Operational Process

The operation process is the map to reaching your destination.
If the map is ignored, it is very easy to get off track.

Operation Analysis

An overview of the operational process provides insight into the end goal of the proposed changes to the business process. This operation provides the customer with a resolution to a problem or fulfills a customer need, most often in the form of a date on which additional services will be performed. This process starts when the customer has a need, the customer contacts The Company seeking resolution. The Company receives that customer desire and transforms it to a scheduled time and date when the final resolution to the desire is fulfilled. This small operation in The Company’s overall business is about providing a service to the customer and achieving the greatest amount of customer satisfaction in the short time the operations lasts.

The current procedures that govern the operation have been evaluated and found to be inadequate for the tasks the customer service representatives are performing on a day to day basis. These procedures should be providing step by step instructions for taking calls and scheduling services, but they do not over all of the circumstances. They do not direct the representatives to transfer the caller to Billing and Accounts when they with to discontinue service. Instead it is up to the local ‘tribal knowledge’ that the call must be transferred. This and other omissions and errors have come about over time when the process has changed without explicitly changing the procedures.

A Word on Quality

The Company strives to maintain efficient and effective operations. Doing so requires continual improvement and evaluation of operational processes. The current business process that manages the operational process has grown to be inefficient as the service area has grown in size. This inefficiency has come, in part, by a lack of constant implementation of procedures at the different regional facilities. With centralization of the customer service representatives new procedures will be developed that are easier to understand and work to. This will improve the implementation of the procedures and improve the efficiency of the overall operation aligning with The Company's goal of Total Quality Managment.
