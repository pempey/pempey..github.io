The Customer is the Key

Continual improvement of the relationship with the customer is crucial.
Customers are the reason businesses succeed.

Continual Improvment

The proposed changes to the business model can not be effective without the customers that are calling in need of service. This process is targeted at improving the customer satisfaction as a way to increase revenue and as such further details and decisions around ways to improve the impact of the change. The process itself will improve the customer satisfaction through uniformity and continuity of service, but there are further refinements that can be had. New training for the customer service representatives can further improve the likelihood of a high increase in customer satisfaction.

The choice of training is not without risk. If a training class is selected there is a chance that it will have a negative impact on increasing the likelihood of an increase in customer satisfaction. At present, there are two training classes available for the customer service representatives; Class A with a 0.6 probability of improving the likelihood from 50% to 70% or a 0.4 probability to decrease it to 45%, and Class B with a 0.8 probability of improving the likelihood to 60% or a 0.2 probability of decreasing it to 40%. There also remains the choice to not do any training at all, allowing the original estimate of 50% stand.

Utility and Risk

With the success of the change on process depending on a significant improvement in the customer satisfaction rating The Company is averse to taking significant risk on decisions that will impact the rating. Applying the aversion to risk as a measure of utility to the decision shows that one class, Class A has a negative utility indicating that it is too risky for The Company to select. The other class, Class B does have a positive utility indicating that the level of risk is acceptable to The Company. The option of providing no additional training is given utility of zero as it is already in place and has a known, and fixed, chance of success. Given the positive utility from Class B, it is recommended that it be selected as a way to improve the customer satisfaction beyond that of the existing options.
