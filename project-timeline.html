Project Timeline and Cost

Rome was not built in a day, and neither are projects.
Knowing the time and cost of implementaion is vital to making the correct decision.

Timeline

The proposed changes will take time and money to implement. Over a period of five years the new systems will be put in place and the staff will be centralized. The regional staff will be centralized over time closing all of the regional support over a period of two and a half years. Project costs are not expected to go below current operational cost until the fourth year of the project, but it is expected that there will be an almost immediate improvement in the customer satisfaction rating when the new system comes on-line.
